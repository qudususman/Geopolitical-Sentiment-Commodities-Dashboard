# 📊 Geopolitical Sentiment & Commodities Intelligence Terminal
![Excel](https://shields.io) ![BI](https://shields.io) ![Status](https://shields.io)
An interactive, multi-dimensional Business Intelligence Terminal engineered to model the structural correlations between global geopolitical risk variables, maritime chokepoint supply shocks, and international spot price volatility for precious metals and energy assets.
## 🛠️ Professional Technical Architecture & Analytics Stack
* **Core Processing Engine:** Microsoft Excel (Advanced Pivot Table Aggregations, Custom Value Field Architectures, and Non-Linear Data Modeling) [17.3].
* **Relational Logic Operations:** Multi-tier conditional data arrays mapping qualitative sentiment tags across discrete historical and real-time temporal frameworks [17.3].
* **Business Intelligence Logic:** Volatility tracking and safe-haven capital migration metrics with integrated variance delta computation.
* **Frontend User Interface (UI):** Cross-linked, dual-themed executive dashboard canvas utilizing floating KPI cards, integrated visual slicers, and multi-variable analytics charts.
## 📊 Core Data Architecture & Visual Metrics
* **Geopolitical Sentiment Indicators:** Interactive Slicer Panel control matrix automating instant, system-wide filtering across key market vibes: *High Tension, Deep Dip, Cooling, and Profit Taking* [17.3].
* **Energy Supply Distortions Axis:** Clustered column charts tracking average oil price impact percentages side-by-side during naval traffic choke bottlenecks [17.3].
* **Precious Metals Capital Velocity Matrix:** High-density safe-haven donut charts illustrating gold spot price distributions during international diplomatic crises [17.3].
* **Macro Risk Trend Line:** Micro-marker visualization pipelines tracking real-time asset trends against active military escalation milestones.
## 📈 Key Strategic Data Insights Captured
* **Geopolitical Fear Risk Premium:** Quantitative data modeling shows that periods tagged as "High Tension" (such as the Strait of Hormuz naval blockade) trigger an immediate, sharp divergence—causing oil prices to spike while gold experiences rapid safe-haven volume accumulation.
* **Inverse Dollar Velocity:** Systematic analysis of the data tracking mechanisms reveals that during temporary diplomatic "Cooling" phases, speculative capital rapidly rotates back out of commodities, creating clear short-term profit-taking dips.
* **Commodity-to-Volume Metrics:** Cross-linked slicer analysis proves that international spot movements are strictly bound to physical supply disruption levels, allowing the terminal to successfully predict price resistance ceilings.
